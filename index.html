Ctrl+Sea

Ctrl+Sea: From River to Coast is a community-driven advocacy blog documenting the fight for cleaner waterways and coastlines across Cebu.

Cebu's rivers are dying and the coast is on the receiving end. 80% of all aquatic plastic pollution originates not from bodies of water but land instead (Bardenas et al., 2023). Cebu is on track to produce more solid waste than any other province in the Philippines, and that waste is already showing up inside the fish that fisherfolk pull from the sea every day as reported by Fabro, 2024.

Cleanups alone aren't enough. Every piece of waste collected is also a data point. Ctrl+Sea turns community action into insight that can push for real change, following the lead of data-driven cleanup programs across the Philippines that have already been proven by some organizations like the Partnership in Environmental Management for the Seas of East Asia (PEMSEA, 2025).

Cebu City rivers: Household waste leading cause of pollution

According to Cebu City Environment and Natural Resources Office (CCENRO), the 2025 River Assessment Report found that Cebu City’s rivers are dying because of household waste. Rivers that have been polluted are the Lahug River, the Mahiga River, the Bulacao River, which are heavily polluted by plastic sachets, untreated gray water, and organic waste, and lastly the Guadalupe River, which is the most polluted waterways in the city. A few rivers such as the Cotcot River still have good ecological conditions and clean water but are vulnerable as population increases near waterways. CCENRO recommends stronger environmental education, better household waste management, better enforcement in environment regulations, and continued river cleanup efforts.

Call Out to Protect City’s Last Clean Rivers

A series of test in December 2024 by CCENRO’s team aiming to identify Cebu City’s rivers that are still clean, assess the river’s health, identify possible threats, and compose recommendations for the future, found that a few rivers still remain intact such as the Cotcot River and parts of the Lusaran River and the Butuanon River. The CCENRO urges that the remaining clean rivers of Cebu City need protection for the health of future generations. They said that protecting these rivers would not just preserve nature but also safeguard human health and livelihoods.

Floating Barriers Installed in Cebu City Rivers to Fight Plastic Waste

On October 24, 2025, floating river blooms were installed in Barangays Bacayan, Bulacao, Inayawan, Labangon, and Pahina Central.

The purpose of these floating river blooms is to trap trash before it contaminates major waterways.

The goal of this project is to make Cebu the first in the Philippines with zero waste flowing into the ocean.

SDG 6 Targets

What We're
Fighting For

6.1
Safe and Affordable Drinking Water

This target aims to ensure that everyone has access to safe, affordable, and reliable drinking water by 2030. It focuses on improving water supply systems, reducing inequalities in access to clean water, and ensuring that communities have a sustainable source of potable water. Projects under this target often involve water quality monitoring, improving water infrastructure, and increasing access to clean water in underserved areas.

6.2
Adequate Sanitation and Hygiene

Target 6.2 seeks to provide equitable access to sanitation and hygiene facilities for all while eliminating open defecation. It also emphasizes the importance of hygiene practices, such as proper handwashing, particularly in schools, healthcare facilities, and public spaces. This target aims to improve public health, reduce the spread of diseases, and promote dignity through better sanitation services.

6.3
Improve Water Quality and Wastewater Management

This target focuses on improving the quality of water by reducing pollution, minimizing the release of hazardous chemicals, decreasing the amount of untreated wastewater, and encouraging the recycling and safe reuse of water. It promotes responsible waste management practices to protect rivers, lakes, and groundwater from contamination. Achieving this target contributes to healthier ecosystems and safer water resources for communities.

6.4
Increase Water-Use Efficiency and Ensure Sustainable Withdrawals

Target 6.4 aims to improve the efficient use of water across households, agriculture, industries, and other sectors while ensuring that freshwater resources are managed sustainably. The goal is to reduce water scarcity by encouraging conservation practices, preventing wastage, and promoting technologies that optimize water consumption. This target supports long-term water security for both people and the environment.

6.5
Implement Integrated Water Resources Management

This target promotes the coordinated management of water resources to balance social, economic, and environmental needs. It encourages governments, organizations, and communities to work together in planning and managing rivers, watersheds, lakes, and groundwater systems. Effective water resource management helps ensure sustainable development while minimizing conflicts over water use.

6.6
Protect and Restore Water-Related Ecosystems

Target 6.6 focuses on conserving and restoring ecosystems that depend on water, including rivers, lakes, wetlands, forests, aquifers, and mountains. Healthy ecosystems play a vital role in maintaining clean water supplies, preventing floods, supporting biodiversity, and regulating the natural water cycle. Protecting these ecosystems helps ensure the long-term sustainability of freshwater resources.
