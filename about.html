The Team

About
Ctrl+Sea

Ctrl+Sea: From River to Coast stands for a simple belief: clean water is a right, not a privilege, and protecting it is something all of us share. That responsibility runs from the rivers in our barangays all the way to our coast. We are first-year college students taking up Software Engineering, and we believe technology and community action belong together. Our advocacy is anchored in UN Sustainable Development Goal 6: Clean Water and Sanitation, which calls for the sustainable management of water for everyone. We focus especially on Target 6.3, which aims to improve water quality by reducing pollution and dumping, and Target 6.6, which works to protect and restore water-related ecosystems like our rivers and coastlines.

The Mission

Every cleanup is more than a cleanup. When waste enters a river in Cebu, it does not stay there. It travels to the sea, breaks down, and eventually returns to us through the fish on our tables. We want to break that cycle at its source. Since most of the plastic polluting our waters actually begins on land, the fight for cleaner seas is really a fight for cleaner communities. We stand for data over guesswork. Every piece of waste collected is a data point, and each one tells a story: what is polluting our waters, where it comes from, and who needs to act. By turning community cleanups into organized information, we help small acts of service become real evidence that can shape policy and push for lasting change. We stand for documentation as advocacy. Change that goes unrecorded is easily forgotten. By documenting the state of Cebu's waterways and the work being done to heal them, we give our community a record, a voice, and a way to ask for accountability. We stand for participation. Clean water cannot be achieved by a few people alone. It takes fisherfolk, students, local government, and everyday citizens working side by side, each giving what they can, whether that is labor, knowledge, or code. Our promise: As students who are just beginning our journey in Software Engineering, we want to use the tools of our field, like data, systems, and technology, in service of our environment and our people. Ctrl+Sea is our first step in showing that even beginners can build something that matters.
